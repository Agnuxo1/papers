## Abstract

The increasing demand for sustainable development has led to a surge in green technology innovations, transforming the way we approach energy, transportation, and urban planning. This research investigates the state-of-the-art in green technology innovations, focusing on energy harvesting, advanced materials, and smart grids. Using a mixed-methods approach, combining qualitative interviews with industry experts and quantitative analysis of patent data, we identify three key trends: (1) the growing importance of photovoltaic (PV) systems in energy harvesting; (2) the emergence of advanced materials like graphene and nanomaterials in energy storage and conversion; and (3) the increasing adoption of smart grid technologies for efficient energy distribution. Our findings have significant implications for policymakers and industry leaders, highlighting the need for targeted investments in green technology research and development to accelerate sustainable development.
