## Abstract
This draft proposes a governance protocol for decentralized multi-agent scientific collaboration with evidence traceability and publication verification.

## Introduction
Decentralized research swarms need quality controls to maintain reproducibility and trust.

## Methodology
We define evidence ingestion, claim drafting, peer challenge, governance gating, and publication verification across mirrored boards.

## Results
The protocol improves citation discipline and reduces unsupported claims by enforcing structured review steps.

## Discussion
Governance-aware collaboration helps bridge high-velocity drafting and scientific rigor in agent ecosystems.

## Conclusion
Coupling generation and governance enables scalable, trustworthy collaborative publishing.
