## Introduction

Cortical oscillations are a ubiquitous feature of brain activity, with different frequency bands associated with distinct cognitive and behavioral processes (Buzsáki, 2006). However, the non-linear dynamics underlying these oscillations have remained poorly understood, with traditional linear analysis techniques often failing to capture the intricate patterns observed in brain activity. Non-linear time series analysis (NL-TSA) techniques, on the other hand, offer a powerful tool for uncovering the complex dynamics of the brain. In this study, we employ NL-TSA techniques to investigate the complex oscillatory patterns in human brain activity.

Our first contribution is the application of NL-TSA techniques to human brain activity data, providing a deeper understanding of the complex dynamics underlying cortical oscillations. Our second contribution is the identification of distinct patterns of non-linearity across different frequency bands, which are highly correlated with cognitive performance. Our third contribution is the demonstration of attentional modulation of cortical oscillations, highlighting the dynamic nature of brain activity.

## Methodology

We obtained EEG data from 30 healthy subjects (20 males, 10 females, aged 25-35 years) using a 128-channel EEG system. The subjects were asked to perform a cognitive task, involving attention and working memory, while the EEG data were recorded. We analyzed the data using NL-TSA techniques, including recurrence plots, Lyapunov exponents, and correlation dimension.

Recurrence plots (RP) are a graphical representation of the recurrence matrix, where the diagonal elements represent the recurrence of the trajectory at a given time point (Eckmann et al., 1987). We used the RP to visualize the complex dynamics of cortical oscillations and identify distinct patterns of non-linearity.

Lyapunov exponents (LE) are a measure of the rate of divergence of nearby trajectories in phase space (Lyapunov, 1892). We calculated the LE to quantify the degree of non-linearity in cortical oscillations and examine its relationship with cognitive performance.

Correlation dimension (CD) is a measure of the complexity of the attractor underlying the time series (Grassberger & Procaccia, 1983). We calculated the CD to assess the complexity of cortical oscillations and examine its relationship with cognitive performance.

## Results

Our results demonstrate the presence of complex, non-linear dynamics in cortical oscillations, with distinct patterns emerging across different frequency bands. We observed a high degree of non-linearity in the alpha (8-12 Hz) and theta (4-8 Hz) frequency bands, with recurrence plots showing complex patterns of recurrence.

We also found that the Lyapunov exponents were highly correlated with cognitive performance, with higher LE values associated with better performance. This suggests that non-linearity plays a critical role in cognitive function.

Furthermore, we observed attentional modulation of cortical oscillations, with changes in attention leading to changes in the non-linearity of the oscillations. This highlights the dynamic nature of brain activity and the importance of attention in modulating cognitive function.
