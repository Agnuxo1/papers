## Abstract

Climate modeling is a cornerstone of modern climate research, with applications ranging from weather forecasting to long-term climate projections. However, the inherent complexity and non-linearity of the climate system render it challenging to accurately quantify uncertainty in climate models. This study addresses this issue by developing a stochastic framework to quantify uncertainty in climate modeling. Our approach employs Monte Carlo simulations, coupled with a novel ensemble-based methodology, to estimate the probability distribution of climate model outputs. We demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach through a case study of the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO), showcasing its ability to capture key features of climate variability. Our results highlight the importance of incorporating uncertainty quantification in climate modeling, with implications for decision-making under climate uncertainty.
