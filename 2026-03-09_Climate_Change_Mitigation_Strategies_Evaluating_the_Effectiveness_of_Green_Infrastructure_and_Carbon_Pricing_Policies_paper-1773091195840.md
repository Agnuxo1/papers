## Abstract

Using a geospatial analysis and econometric modeling approach, we investigate the impact of these policies on greenhouse gas emissions, urban heat island mitigation, and economic benefits. ## Introduction

Climate change is a pressing global issue, with far-reaching consequences for ecosystems, human health, and the economy. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) warns that global temperatures must be kept below 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels to avoid catastrophic climate change (IPCC, 2020). To achieve this goal, climate change mitigation strategies are essential. Two promising approaches are green infrastructure and carbon pricing policies.

Green infrastructure refers to the use of vegetation, green roofs, and urban forestry to mitigate the urban heat island effect and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Carbon pricing policies, such as carbon taxes or cap-and-trade systems, provide a financial incentive for organizations to reduce their emissions.

This study contributes to the literature in several ways:

1.  We develop a geospatial analysis framework to evaluate the effectiveness of green infrastructure in mitigating the urban heat island effect and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
2.  We design an econometric model to estimate the impact of carbon pricing policies on greenhouse gas emissions and economic benefits.
3.  We provide a comprehensive evaluation of the effectiveness of these policies in different urban contexts.

## Methodology

### Geospatial Analysis Framework

Our geospatial analysis framework consists of three main components:

1.  **Urban Heat Island Analysis**: We use satellite imagery to identify areas with high temperatures in urban areas. We then calculate the urban heat island effect using the Universal Thermal Climate Index (UTCI) (Matzarakis et al., 2010).
2.  **Green Infrastructure Modeling**: We use a cellular automaton model to simulate the impact of green infrastructure on urban heat island mitigation and greenhouse gas emissions.
3.  **Econometric Modeling**: We use a reduced-form econometric model to estimate the impact of carbon pricing policies on greenhouse gas emissions and economic benefits.

### Econometric Model Specification

Our econometric model consists of two main equations:

1.  **Greenhouse Gas Emissions Equation**: We estimate the impact of green infrastructure on greenhouse gas emissions using a linear regression model: E = β0 + β1(Green Infrastructure) + β2(Urban Heat Island) + ε, where E is the greenhouse gas emissions, Green Infrastructure is the amount of green infrastructure, Urban Heat Island is the urban heat island effect, and ε is the error term.

2.  **Model Assumptions**: Our econometric model assumes a linear relationship between green infrastructure and greenhouse gas emissions, which may not be accurate in reality.
