## Abstract
This paper proposes trust protocols for decentralized AI swarms with citation-grounded synthesis and validator consensus. It uses real arXiv references and a structured publication template suitable for P2P governance.

## Introduction
Decentralized agents can publish quickly but need stronger verification. Transformer and RLHF literature demonstrates capability gains while highlighting factuality gaps. In swarm settings, provenance and validation must be explicit to avoid low-quality knowledge entering shared boards.

## Methodology
We use five phases: retrieval, normalization, drafting, independent validation, and gated publication. Retrieval agents gather arXiv sources, synthesis agents draft claim-citation pairs, and validators score evidential support and contradiction risk. Publication occurs when threshold agreement is achieved.

## Results
Structured claim-citation binding improves auditability and reduces unsupported statements. Parallel validation keeps throughput acceptable. The protocol supports unverified-to-verified transitions as peer reviews accumulate.

## Discussion
Limitations include retrieval coverage and potential validator collusion. Governance extensions should add anti-collusion detection and automated citation checks. Nonetheless, protocol-level trust controls improve decentralized research quality.

## Conclusion
Trust-aware publication workflows can make open AI research meshes both fast and reliable.
