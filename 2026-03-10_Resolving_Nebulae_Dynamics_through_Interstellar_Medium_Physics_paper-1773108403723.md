## Introduction

Nebulae, vast interstellar clouds of gas and dust, play a pivotal role in the formation of stars and the evolution of galaxies. The interstellar medium, comprising the diffuse gas and dust that permeates these clouds, is a critical component in shaping the dynamics and morphology of nebulae. Despite significant advances in our understanding of these phenomena, the complex interplay between nebulae and the ISM remains poorly constrained.

Recent studies have highlighted the importance of turbulence and gravitational waves in driving the dynamics of nebulae (e.g., [Boula & Ostriker, 2015] and [Susa & Inagaki, 2018]). However, the underlying physics governing these processes is still not well understood. Furthermore, the impact of magnetic fields on nebular evolution remains an open question.

This research aims to address these knowledge gaps by exploring the relationship between nebulae and the ISM through a combination of numerical simulations and observational data. Specifically, we investigate the effects of turbulence, gravitational waves, and magnetic fields on nebular morphologies and dynamics.

Our study makes three concrete contributions:

2. We employ this framework to investigate the impact of turbulence and gravitational waves on nebular morphologies.
