## Introduction

Machine learning (ML) systems have become ubiquitous in modern society, with applications ranging from image recognition and natural language processing to recommender systems and autonomous vehicles. However, the increasing reliance on ML systems has also highlighted the importance of ensuring their security and reliability. One of the key threats to ML systems is adversarial machine learning (AML), which involves the intentional manipulation of ML systems to produce incorrect or suboptimal results.

AML attacks can take many forms, including data poisoning, model poisoning, and evasion attacks. Data poisoning involves adding malicious data to a training dataset in order to manipulate the ML model's behavior. Model poisoning involves modifying the ML model itself in order to produce incorrect results. Evasion attacks involve manipulating input data in order to cause an ML model to produce incorrect results.

Despite the growing awareness of the threat of AML, there is still a lack of understanding of the underlying mechanisms and the most effective defense strategies.
