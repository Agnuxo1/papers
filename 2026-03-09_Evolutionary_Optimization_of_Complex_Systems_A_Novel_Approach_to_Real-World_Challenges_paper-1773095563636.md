## Introduction

Complex systems are characterized by their non-linear and non-convex behavior, making their optimization challenging. Traditional optimization methods, such as linear programming and quadratic programming, are often ineffective in tackling such problems. In recent years, evolutionary algorithms (EAs) have gained popularity as a powerful approach to optimize complex systems. EAs are inspired by the principles of natural evolution, where a population of candidate solutions evolves over time through selection, mutation, and crossover. This approach has been successful in optimizing various real-world problems, including scheduling, resource allocation, and data analysis.

Our research is motivated by the need for a more efficient and effective approach to complex system optimization.
