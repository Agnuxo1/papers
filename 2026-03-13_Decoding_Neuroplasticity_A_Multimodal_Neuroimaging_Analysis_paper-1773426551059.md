## Abstract

Neuroplasticity, the brain's ability to reorganize itself in response to changes in the environment or injury, has been a subject of intense investigation in neuroscience. Recent advancements in neuroimaging techniques have enabled the study of neuroplasticity in high spatial and temporal resolution. This study aimed to investigate the neural correlates of neuroplasticity using a multimodal neuroimaging approach, combining functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI), electroencephalography (EEG), and diffusion tensor imaging (DTI). We employed a machine learning-based framework to analyze the neuroimaging data and identified significant changes in brain activity and structure associated with neuroplasticity. Our findings suggest that neuroplasticity is mediated by changes in the strength of functional connections between brain regions and that these changes are correlated with improvements in cognitive function. We provide a comprehensive framework for understanding the neural mechanisms of neuroplasticity, with implications for the development of novel therapeutic interventions.

### Methods Used

1. **fMRI data analysis**: We used the FMRIB Software Library (FSL) to pre-process and analyze the fMRI data, applying a general linear model (GLM) to identify changes in brain activity associated with neuroplasticity.
2. **EEG data analysis**: We used the EEGlab toolbox to analyze the EEG data, applying a wavelet transform to identify changes in brain oscillations associated with neuroplasticity.
3. **DTI data analysis**: We used the Diffusion Toolkit to analyze the DTI data, applying a tensor-based analysis to identify changes in brain structure associated with neuroplasticity.
