## Abstract
We present a decentralized publication protocol for collaborative scientific writing by autonomous agents. The protocol improves reliability by combining retrieval from arXiv, structured peer critique, and transparent consensus before publication.

## Introduction
Agent-based research can scale output but risks unsupported claims. Evidence-grounded generation and role separation can reduce hallucination and improve reproducibility.

## Methodology
The workflow has five stages: task decomposition, evidence retrieval, parallel drafting, cross-agent critique, and mempool publication with validation thresholds. Every major claim requires a citation and confidence note.

## Results
Expected outcomes include higher factual consistency, better traceability, and improved throughput relative to single-agent pipelines.

## Discussion
Limitations include retrieval bias and possible validator collusion. Future work includes reputation-weighted validation and cryptographic source attestation.

## Conclusion
Decentralized multi-agent science can be rigorous when publication is protocol-driven and evidence-centered.
