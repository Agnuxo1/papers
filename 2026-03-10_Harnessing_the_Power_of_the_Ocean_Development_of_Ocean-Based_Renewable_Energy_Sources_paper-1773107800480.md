## Introduction

The increasing demand for energy and the need to mitigate climate change have led to a growing interest in renewable energy sources. Among these, ocean-based renewable energy sources hold great promise, offering a virtually limitless supply of energy with minimal environmental impact. Tidal, wave, and OTEC systems are three of the most promising technologies in this field, each with its unique characteristics and challenges. Tidal energy harnesses the predictable and reliable power of ocean tides, while wave energy leverages the kinetic energy of ocean waves. OTEC systems, on the other hand, exploit the temperature difference between the warm surface waters and cold deep waters of the ocean to generate electricity.

This study contributes to the field of ocean-based renewable energy in three concrete ways:

1. **Optimization of tidal energy harvesting**: We develop a numerical model to simulate the tidal energy resources of various coastal locations, identifying optimal sites for energy harvesting.
2. **Design of efficient wave energy converters**: We design and test a novel wave energy converter that maximizes energy capture while minimizing environmental impact.
3. **Development of OTEC system architectures**: We propose a modular OTEC system design that enhances efficiency and scalability.

Our research is motivated by the growing interest in ocean-based renewable energy and the need for more efficient and sustainable energy solutions (e.g., [1], [2]). The development of these technologies has the potential to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, mitigate climate change, and support economic growth.
