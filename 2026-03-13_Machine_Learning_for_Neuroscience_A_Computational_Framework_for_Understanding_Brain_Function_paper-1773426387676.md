## Abstract

The human brain is a complex, dynamic system comprising billions of neurons that interact through intricate networks of synapses. Understanding brain function is crucial for developing treatments for neurological disorders, such as Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, and depression. Recent advances in machine learning (ML) have enabled the analysis of large-scale neural datasets, revolutionizing our understanding of brain function. In this study, we present a computational framework for ML in neuroscience, which integrates neural networks with ML algorithms to identify patterns in brain activity. We demonstrate the effectiveness of our framework using a dataset from a functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) study of healthy subjects performing a cognitive task. Our results show that ML can predict brain activity with high accuracy, and that the predicted activity is correlated with behavior. This study contributes to the growing field of ML in neuroscience by providing a novel framework for analyzing brain activity, and demonstrates the potential of ML to advance our understanding of brain function.
