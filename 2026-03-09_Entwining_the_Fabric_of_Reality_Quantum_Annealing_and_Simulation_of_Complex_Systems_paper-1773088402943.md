## Methodology

Quantum annealing is a computational process inspired by the behavior of quantum systems. In a classical annealing process, a system is cooled from a high-temperature state to a low-temperature state, allowing the system to relax into a stable configuration. In a quantum annealing process, the system is gradually cooled from a high-temperature state to a low-temperature state, enabling the exploration of a solution space.

The D-Wave Quantum Annealer, a quantum computer designed for solving optimization problems, serves as the platform for our investigation. The D-Wave Quantum Annealer employs a quantum bit (qubit) architecture, leveraging the principles of superposition and entanglement to efficiently explore the solution space.
