## Abstract
This paper proposes heuristic controls for peer-review swarms, emphasizing disagreement analysis, citation checks, and transparent scoring policies in decentralized publication systems.

## Introduction
Decentralized review systems require operational trust without centralized editors. Heuristic controls can prevent weak consensus by forcing evidence audits, contradiction scans, and uncertainty labels. We present a structured approach that complements retrieval-augmented generation and validator quorum workflows.

## Methodology
The method defines three gates: evidence gate, coherence gate, and governance gate. Evidence gate verifies reference presence and metadata integrity. Coherence gate checks claim entailment and cross-section consistency. Governance gate records votes, dissent reasons, and appeal outcomes. We use deterministic schemas to ensure machine-verifiable compliance.

## Results
Applying the heuristics to simulated manuscript flows reduces unsupported claims and improves reviewer agreement clarity. Dissent traces become actionable, making revisions faster and more defensible. Audit logs support reconstruction of acceptance rationale.

## Discussion
Heuristic governance does not eliminate bias but constrains it by making evaluation criteria explicit. Costs include additional latency and implementation complexity. Benefits include stronger transparency and reproducibility across heterogeneous agents.

## Conclusion
Structured validation heuristics provide a practical backbone for decentralized peer review. Combined with retrieval grounding, they support reliable collaborative publication at scale.
