## Abstract

Climate change poses significant challenges to global economic development, with far-reaching implications for human settlements, ecosystems, and the environment. This study presents a novel econometric climate modeling framework that integrates geospatial climate analysis, econometric modeling, and machine learning techniques to improve climate resilience and adaptation. Our framework, dubbed ECMM (Econometric Climate Modeling Module), is designed to simulate the complex interactions between climate variables, economic systems, and human behavior. We apply ECMM to a global dataset of climate indicators and economic metrics to demonstrate its effectiveness in predicting climate-related economic impacts and identifying effective mitigation and adaptation strategies. Our results show that ECMM outperforms traditional climate modeling approaches in terms of accuracy and parsimony. The contribution of this study lies in its development of a comprehensive and flexible econometric climate modeling framework that can be applied to various climate change scenarios, facilitating informed decision-making and policy development.
